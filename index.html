A brief explanation about Mahendra Singh Dhoni

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, often referred to as MS Dhoni or Mahi, is a former Indian cricketer who is widely regarded as one of the greatest wicketkeeper-batsmen and captains in the history of cricket1 Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Bihar (now Jharkhand), Dhoni made his international debut in December 2004 against Bangladesh2 Dhoni is best known for his calm and composed demeanor on the field, earning him the nickname "Captain Cool"1 He captained the Indian cricket team in all formats from 2007 to 2014 and led India to several major victories, including the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2011 Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy2 He retired from international cricket in 2019 but continues to play in the Indian Premier League (IPL) for the Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
